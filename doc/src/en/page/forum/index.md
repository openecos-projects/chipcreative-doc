# ChipCreative Forum
## Introduction
ChipCreative Forum (hereinafter referred to as "the Forum") is a semi-academic conference/event founded and operated by the ECOS Team, **aimed at sharing the latest developments in China's open-source chip technology and ecosystem.** Unlike traditional academic conferences (such as the four top-tier computer architecture conferences ASPLOS/HPCA/ISCA/MICRO) or technical summits (such as the RISC-V Summit), the Forum has two distinctive features: **First, extremely low participation barriers and costs.** No academic background or practical experience in computer-related fields is required; any enthusiast interested in open-source chips can submit reports for free or participate in offline discussions. **Second, a commitment to the purest form of technical exchange.** Participants are all frontline researchers, industry engineers, students, or enthusiasts who may know nothing about chip technology (the ECOS Team considers such users highly valuable, as they often contribute the most genuine needs and creative ideas). Discussions directly address the core issues of open-source chip technology development and deployment, completely eliminating lengthy, content-free "formalism." Through this community-oriented organization, the Forum strives to create a pure, relaxed atmosphere where every participant is **"willing to share and happy to share"**, jointly driving open-source chips in the right direction.

## Background
The core purpose of the ECOS Team in establishing the Forum is **to build a platform that showcases China's open-source chip achievements to the outside world, and to collaborate with domestic and international teams to jointly promote the development of the open-source chip ecosystem.** As mentioned in *The Art of War* by Sun Tzu: **"Know yourself and know your enemy, and you will never be defeated."** The ECOS Team believes that for open-source chips to become a true driving force capable of influencing or even leading future technological development, we must first achieve "knowing ourselves"—systematically analyzing and summarizing our own achievements, experiences, and ideas in this field. Then, through technical exchanges or knowledge sharing, we enable teams with similar research interests at home and abroad to understand each other deeply—achieving "knowing the enemy"—thus laying a solid foundation for subsequent industry-university-research cooperation. **Being a catalyst that encourages individual progress and enables extensive exchanges and collaboration—that is the greatest significance and value of the ChipCreative Forum.** We hope that one day, the ChipCreative Forum will stand alongside conferences such as FSiC and ORConf in Europe, Latch-Up in North America, and Down Underflow in Oceania, telling the world the stories of open-source chips from China, Asia, and the world.

## Goal
The Forum focuses on all areas related to open-source chip technology or ecosystems, such as open-source IP/SoC, open-source EDA, open-source PDK, open-source hardware (PCB design and embedded applications), open-source tapeout, chip design platforms, advanced packaging, novel chips, talent cultivation, and community building. From the dimensions of value and influence, the ECOS Team has outlined a preliminary vision and roadmap for the Forum's future development, as detailed below:

- **Focus on China (Three Years):** Mainly targeting domestic open-source chip communities and enthusiasts. By organizing high-quality technical forums and a series of concurrent activities, we will explore the best way to build China's open-source chip technology and ecosystem, meeting the exchange and collaboration needs of domestic research institutions, universities, and communities in theoretical and technical exploration. Priority will be given to supporting developers in completing the design, verification, and tapeout of open-source chips in a local environment. This stage focuses domestically, so the Forum's format will not pursue grandeur or cumbersome processes, but rather concentrate on opening up the entire open-source chip technology chain.
- **Connect Asia (Three Years):** Mainly targeting Asian open-source chip communities and enthusiasts. Based on the experience gained in the "Focus on China" stage, we will attract or invite open-source chip teams from other Asian countries or regions to participate in the Forum, enhancing collaboration and innovation capabilities in Asia's open-source chip field. Part of our effort will shift to building regional open standards and cooperation, including platform development, commercial deployment, and talent cultivation, enabling Asian developers to complete the full chip design process at a lower cost.
- **Go Global (Five Years):** Mainly targeting global open-source chip communities and enthusiasts. Building on the successful cases and international collaboration models gathered during the "Connect Asia" stage, we will further attract or invite experts, representatives, and enthusiasts from the global open-source chip field to participate in the Forum. Focusing on the first-mover advantages and technological achievements of different countries or regions in open-source chips, we will promote a series of implementable and reproducible typical solutions, gradually facilitating the eventual establishment of a "Belt and Road" cooperation mechanism in the open-source chip field. This will allow users worldwide to experience the benefits of open-source technology at a lower threshold.

## Terms
The Forum advocates an **open, inclusive, and pragmatic** communication environment. Participants should adhere to basic codes of conduct when sharing and discussing technology during the Forum and on community channels. **It is strictly forbidden to publish or disseminate any content that violates laws or regulations, misleading or false statements, or any form of discrimination or personal attack.** The ECOS Team reserves the right to intervene in actions that violate community guidelines (including but not limited to revoking participation qualifications, removing offending content, adding to a blacklist, etc.) in order to maintain the purity and professionalism of open-source technical exchanges, ensuring that every member can grow in an atmosphere of respect and mutual assistance. At the same time, each participant must consciously uphold the brand reputation of the Forum and shall not use any means to defame or tarnish it; violators will also be penalized.

Regarding intellectual property and legal risks: **The brand, organizational planning, and related streaming media copyrights of the ChipCreative Forum belong to the ECOS Team, while the copyrights of presentations, technical documentation, and speech content belong to the individual speakers or their affiliated institutions.** Without explicit written authorization from the ECOS Team or the original authors, no institution or individual can use the Forum's content for commercial purposes, or re-edit and mirror it. If such actions are discovered, the ECOS Team reserves the right to pursue legal remedies. In addition, the technical cases and solutions shared at the Forum are for reference and exchange only. The Forum organizers assume no responsibility for legal risks or losses arising from direct or indirect use of the related technologies. Developers are expected to follow relevant open-source licenses and compliance requirements when applying them.
