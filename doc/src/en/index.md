# Home
## Introduction
ECOS ChipCreative (hereinafter referred to as "the Community") is an open-source community entity brand meticulously crafted by the ECOS Team, and is also one of the leading platforms in China for promoting and organizing the open-source chip ecosystem. The Community deeply revolves around four core areas: **Open Source EDA, Chip Design, One Student One Chip, and System Solutions.** Through **brand operations** it builds influence for open-source projects, relies on **content creation** to produce high-quality documentation, leverages **publication and distribution** to expand existing ecosystem boundaries, and employs **organizational management** to improve development efficiency and quality, thereby constructing a comprehensive support system for open-source chips. The Community is committed to lowering the barrier to chip design, accelerating the openness and collaboration of underlying software and hardware technologies, and providing full-chain support for open-source chip enthusiasts—from design tools to application solutions. At the same time, by integrating high-quality resources from academia and industry, the Community continuously optimizes the soil of the open-source chip ecosystem, speeds up the democratization of technology, and promotes the widespread application and deep participation of local teams/communities in the global open-source chip field, enabling more enthusiasts to achieve technological breakthroughs and create value within a vibrant ecosystem.

## Background
The purpose of the ECOS Team in establishing this Community is **to create a practice-oriented community culture that truly brings the open-source chip ecosystem from behind the scenes to the forefront.** In today's era of rapid iteration of open-source chip technology, a very common "techno-supremacy" mindset still exists within both academia and industry—**overly focusing on technical details while neglecting the importance of brand building, user experience, and ecosystem promotion.** Many excellent open-source projects, due to a lack of professional dissemination methods, outstanding visual systems, efficient organizational management, and other necessary elements, remain at the very end of the influence spectrum or, worse, completely unknown. This not only causes unnecessary waste of resources but also greatly undermines the confidence of developers. Therefore, the Community believes that: **the true explosion of the open-source chip revolution requires not only developers with architecture design skills (code can be written by agents) but also community creators and operators who know how to engage with users.** Developers solve the question of whether a technology/service/product is usable, while community creators and operators solve the question of how to make them better used. The two are interdependent and indispensable. To this end, the Community is committed to building a bridge between technology R&D and brand operations, jointly promoting the prosperity of the open-source chip ecosystem.

## Goal
Drawing on the operational models of other open-source communities or foundations, the ECOS Team has defined three clear evolutionary stages for the Community's development goals. The first stage aims to **disseminate and consolidate open-source chip achievements (Forum & Magazine)** , the second stage aims to **cultivate chip talent and promote technological iteration (Competitions)** , and the third stage aims to **popularize open-source chip culture and reach beyond the core community (Fun Activities)**. The detailed breakdown of these three stages is as follows:

- **Forum & Magazine:** Mainly targeted at open-source chip enthusiasts. Through annually organized technical forums and the publication of magazines/books, the goal is to systematically collate and promote the current stage achievements in the global open-source chip field. The focus of this stage is to build a knowledge system, break down information silos and technology walls through the organic combination of print and digital media, and promote the widespread dissemination of open-source chip design paradigms based on open-source IP/SoC, open-source PDK, and open-source EDA, laying a solid foundation for the rapid development and large-scale expansion of the open-source chip ecosystem.
- **Competitions:** Mainly targeted at open-source chip enthusiasts and university students. Inspired by the organizational model of the RoboCup robot world cup, the goal is to create a pure, world-class open-source chip challenge. By setting targeted technical problems, **the aim is to incentivize the transfer of advanced technologies from industry and academia to the open-source community, testing the reliability of open-source SoC designs and automation toolchains in real-world scenarios.** This stage is dedicated to discovering and cultivating top chip talent, promoting learning and usage through competition, and significantly enhancing the practical value of open-source chip solutions.
- **Fun Activities:** Mainly targeted at global software and hardware developers, makers, and youth groups. Drawing on the Maker Faire and Maker Camp models of the Make: community, the goal is to create a global carnival and immersive learning experience in the open-source chip field. Through large-scale offline interactive exhibitions and fun bootcamps, the aim is to lower the entry barrier to chip design and bring open-source chip culture to the general public. This stage seeks to build a low-threshold knowledge sharing platform, enabling the open-source chip revolution to truly move from the lab to society, allowing users worldwide to experience the benefits of open-source technology.

## Contribution
The Community warmly welcomes every partner who is passionate about open-source culture, creative dissemination, and organizational management. If you excel in writing, video editing, visual design, community operations, event planning, English translation, or similar areas, this is an excellent stage for you to showcase your talents. **By consistently upholding a scientific and rigorous work attitude, pursuing extreme perfection, possessing the will to explore unknown territories, and bringing forth unique ideas, we tell the open-source chip stories of ourselves and our friends, giving warmth to otherwise cold knowledge.** This is the value of the Community, and it is also the trait we hope you possess. Finally, ECOS ChipCreative promises to build a fair and transparent access mechanism. Regardless of your professional background, as long as you identify with the open-source spirit and are willing to contribute to the open-source chip ecosystem, the Community's doors will always be open to you.
